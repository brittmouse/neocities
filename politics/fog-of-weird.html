The New President and the Fog of Weird

January 22, 2025

How We Feeling, Chat?

Bad. The answer is bad.

I'm just so damn tired.

Basically, I've been utterly exhausted since 7 October 2023. I haven't been out protesting or putting up signs or waving flags around. I've been watching the American Left back a right-wing terrorist group for over a year and shout absolutely heinous antisemitic rhetoric. I've had to watch Israel flatten the Gaza Strip and (just recently) launch a major incursion into Jenin in the West Bank, killing at least 9 so far.1 I don't see rhetoric drawing a line between support for civilians and support for either military. Despite the growing voices for a real peace, the loudest ones are activists intensely angry at the other side.

Also, Trump is president again, and wow the fascism is on full display. Media corporations are already censoring themselves and their users. Trump has defined every transgender person out of existence (by accidentally defining everyone as "female"). He launched a goddamn memecoin that was briefly worth $70 billion on 19 January.

What the Fuck is Happening Anymore?

The "uncanny feeling" I've been feeling over 2024 is particularly strong now, and I've seen this with other people. Nothing feels real and because I'm not the only person experiencing this, that just has me more worried. I barely have any idea what's going on anymore.

I can still think the actual circumstances through and try interpreting them but none of it makes much sense.

Fascism and Capital accumulation

The answer I've seen bounced around a lot in left-wing circles, from the DSA to Vaush, is that this is the result of the long slide towards fascism and the accumulation of capital among the ruling class throwing their support behind it. This makes sense through the Marxist framework, as the ruling class stands to gain more money and power throwing their support behind the man who says he's going to make things much better for big business. The Biden administration was emphatically fighting against corporate mergers like those between Kroger and Albertson's grocery store chains and the anti-monopoly attacks against Google. Big tech especially saw this and decided they would have more chanced for money hoarding with a fascist in charge.

Marxist analysis does lack somewhat in explaining why the working and middle classes went along with it though. There's the "class reductionist" perspective, which sacrifices all nuance of the situation into class analysis by saying "This was a deliberate move by the ruling class's control over media to bring a fascist into power that would be good for their wallets." However, I believe this overlooks the media environment split among the poor and the young especially. No one watches NBC or CBS. Old boomers watch Fox News and MSNBC plenty, and they usually have the highest turnouts in elections. The young (which I'm defining here as 18-29) went largely for Harris but only made up 13 percent of the vote.2 That's below even the 30-39 turnout.

The gender split between Gen Z men and women is also stark, and has been commented on plenty in the past several months. This is largely due to what kinds of alternative media young voters are consuming, and I think that may also be the influence around the Fog of Weird that's permeating everything.

Fog of Weird

I've bounced back and forth on what to call this: a mass unreality / derealization event, the fallout of post-irony, the long-COVID effect. November Kelly of TrashFuture and Well, There's Your Problem has been calling this the "Years of Lead Poisoning," which is pretty good but largely focuses on the spat of right-wing terror attacks this past year, probably to set off the Boogaloo. I'm calling it the Fog of Weird.

The Fog of Weird is this persistent, widely felt feeling of nothing making any sense and everything feeling fake and wrong. Everything is self-contradicting, insincere, and cheap. Everything's a scam, there's no hope of getting ahead, advertisements are insultingly stupid and worthless, politics has stopped making sense, and trying to comprehend any of the international politics out there is like trying to lasso a swarm of hornets.

It's a stark separation between reality as it is being perceived and what reality is supposed to be.

Remember the panic over drones in New Jersey? How you had loads of people seeing red and green lights in the sky and panicking over being surveilled by Chinese drones? Remember how there were people demanding a federal investigation into the constellation Orion, that big guy we see in the fall and winter in the Northern Hemisphere? You know how it just stopped one day and we never heard about it again? That is a manifestation of the Fog of Weird.

I'm not sure what's really behind the Fog of Weird. It's just something I've noticed getting worse and worse over the past year and it shows no signs of letting up. It truly feels as though we've entered the Twilight Zone. I'm not sure how this started but I'm not liking its effects.

"Is It Just a Me Thing?" Asked Everyone You Know

I've considered this just being a me feeling for a while but I'm certain this is being widely felt. I've talked with my manager about this feeling a couple of times and he's felt the same thing. Talking with some friends has yielded the same results. Everything just feels off.

This could be anything from long COVID effects, the exhaustion of the past year, or just work stress getting to everyone. But I also wonder about what mental illnesses I'm dealing with. Being trans with depression and anxiety is already a cocktail of suffering, but I'm certain it's worse than just that for me. I've been handling this without meds or therapy for years, just relying on what small self-care stuff I know and leaning heavily on my friends. It's not a great way to do it and I wouldn't recommend doing it this way in the slightest.

We're in for a rough 2 years at least, probably far worse in the foreseeable future. The climate crisis spins on; tech CEOs become feudal lords of the World Wide Web with AI-assisted surveillance and more data collection; products are made worse, made to spy on you, and made by more impoverished workers; the liberal international order that held a semblance of peace together for decades is dying in Ukraine, Gaza, and Taiwan; drug use snared another generation of lonely, desperate young people.

I said back in November that we all had to live. I was right and I'm still going to do that. But fuck me this is discouraging. I'm going to be retreating into this website a lot, which means I probably need to do a reskin of this site. I'm also going to be doing the small things I can do to make the end of it all a little sweeter. Running stream for my temple, trying out Jekyll for a new county interfaith alliance website, volunteering and helping others. I doubt the Fog of Weird is going to lift anytime soon, or if nuclear detonations are going to blow it all clear.

Just make sure to do something that the Fog can't get into. Running this site has helped a lot with my head and even if Neocities goes, I'll make this site come back. Let each breath I take and each day this stays active revive a spark of resistance in my heart and kindle the love in my soul.
