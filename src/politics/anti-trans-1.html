Fighting for My Sanity: Anti-Trans Rhetoric in 2024

October 20, 2024

Election Update

So, we are a little over 2 weeks out right now from the 2024 election, and I'm really ready for this to be over. I went to vote on the first day of polls being opened, which apparently was a real record breaker for ballots cast on the first day! That does give me some hope, as it shows citizens are getting engaged and paying a lot more attention to politics this year. Republican officials were quick to point out that this puts a hole in the argument that Georgia's election laws are "Jim Crow 2.0," as progressives have said that these laws (especially voter I.D. and voter roll purges) unfairly targeted African American citizens. Personally, I'm going to wait until I get more info on the stats of who voted as well as the larger voting patterns in the state as early voting continues and the final results.

Despite this good news at the health of Georgia's voter base, it hasn't all been great news. The Trump-dominated State Election Board has made several new rules that severely fuck up the electoral process, like mandating a hand-count of all ballots in a county to be completed by three poll workers the night of the election (this would lead to a lot of errors just due to a natural human deficiency in counting) and allowing a county election official to object to the certification of votes in their precinct based on a "reasonable inquiry" without defining reasonable.

One of those S.E.B. officials said later "we didn't think we had to define what 'reasonable' means," which is the "I didn't study" answer you could have possibly given. Every political science professor in twenty miles just sighed.

A judge did strike down these rules down because a) they were published too close to the election, and b) for being illegal, specifically the "refuse to certify" rule as that conflicted with a law that says precinct officials "shall" certify the election results by the Monday evening after the election (or the Tuesday evening after if the Monday is a holiday like Veteran's Day). That "shall" leaves no room for an election denier's personal judgement that the majority black county's election is actually unfairly Democratic-leaning and to cancel the results.

Violent Rhetoric

Listen to any Trump speech the past few weeks (and it is Trump speaking, I'm half-convinced Vance is just dead), and he's been a mix of slurred words, nonsense rambles, and intense fear mongering. Between calling immigrants a "poison" to America's bloodstream, wanting to use the military to deport over 10 million undocumented migrants (claiming the number is as high as 25 million to rile up his base even more), and declaring he would revoke ABC's broadcast license for daring to fact-check him in a debate where Trump agreed to let moderators fact-check.

The willingness to lie and invoke increasingly violent imagery suggests two possibilities.

  1. He has completely lost it and it spewing random hateful rhetoric because his brain has rotted.
  2. He's still self-aware and is engaging in this rhetoric hoping that overly scared crowds will look to him as a savior and get him the votes he needs to become the 47th president (or possibly support extrajudicial violence against liberal voters and engage in more election fuckery).

As much as I want to blame this on his brain turning into soup, this feels entirely too calculated to be the nonsense throes of a madman's last years. Granted, he does have "soup brain" moments fairly frequently, but the idea that he's using this sort of language because he lost it doesn't seem to fit with reality.

The Fucking Trans Ads

And this is where I talk about the spat of awful anti-trans ads that the Trump campaign has been running the past few weeks.

If you've managed to avoid the ads while watching college football this year, first, I am deeply jealous; second, the ads hinge largely on "Kamala Harris using taxpayer dollars to do sexual reassignment surgeries on illegal MS-13 drug traffickers." No I'm not kidding. During the ad, Harris is depicted going easy on traffickers and rapists, and then talking with a drag queen about transition care for prisoners, before ending with "Kamala is for they/them; Donald Trump is for YOU."

If this were a more coherent ad, I would be less mad about it. Previous ads (including these) said Harris had gone too easy on immigrants and gang members and that she had let too many go without putting them in prison. These ads now claim that she did put those people in jail but only to do sex changes on all of them using taxpayer money. It's the sort of incoherence of the broader messaging that seems to appeal to Trump voters, because the dumb ones who already vote for him don't really need a coherent argument, they just need to hate trannies harder.

This stuff hurts because it relies on Americans being stupid about policy and the basic functions of government, which unfortunately is a safe bet. Hell, ask a Green Party or PSL voter why they're voting for Jill Stein or Claudia de la Cruz, and they will very quickly reveal that they don't know how the separation of powers work or the difference between state and federal policy over things like abortion and HRT access. Americans just don't pay attention to lackluster civics classes in high school, and I don't find it very likely that C-grade business administration students are going to pay attention in their mandatory American Government class.

So, an explainer is in order.

First, prisons using taxpayer money to fund the operation of the prison. That's how public prisons work. They are owned and operated by the government. If they weren't using government funds to operate, we'd have a much larger problem on our hands – exactly where they're getting tens of millions of dollars without public accountability.

Second, prisoners need healthcare. A correctional institution must provide medical care to patients, no matter what they have (be it diabetes, malnourishment, depression, or even broken bones); otherwise, refusing to provide that medical care would fall under "cruel and unusual punishment" of inmates, which is prohibited under the Eighth Amendment to the U.S. Constitution.

Third, gender-affirming care is medically necessary. Gender affirming healthcare for transgender people drastically lowers the chance of mental illness and suicidality. Despite attempts by conservatives to demonize and pathologize all trans people as sick, degenerate, and predatory, the facts remain that this healthcare, including hormone access, counseling, and gender-affirming surgeries, is life-saving and works.

Fourth, these sorts of procedures have happened during the Trump administration, and it makes absolutely no sense to say Harris is a dirty Marxist (along with Obama, Bill Clinton, and probably Eisenhower at this rate) for getting healthcare to transgender inmates when it's not even new. Granted, Trump supporters aren't generally smart or in the know about anything but it still bugs me.

Fifth, the argument just doesn't make much sense? Like, what does the Biden administration have to gain from performing sex changes on MS-13 operatives? I get the argument is that Democrats just hate White Americans and that only God-Emperor Trump will Protect the White Race, but it still comes across as pathetic and desperate.

On a personal level the ads aggravate me, and that's because Republicans, being either ill-informed or lying, decided years ago to conflate trans women with drag queens, when these are not the same groups of people. There is overlap and a long historical interconnectedness but we are not the same. Like ideally the GOP would just stop hating trans people and drag queens, and there really isn't a point for them to differentiate between two different groups if it harms their messaging. Besides, I really doubt that a GOP voter is going to give a single fuck about the complexities of the topic when they can't even get around the idea of gay sex. Largely, this isn't even about the GOP ads specifically, but the broader social norm that conflates transgender women and drag queens to the point of each group being identical. This probably deserves a separate look but the conflation is intensely aggravating to me considering I really don't like drag and feel no small level of annoyance for being lumped in with them. This isn't a "pick me, I'm the good one" argument, as I'm under no pretense that society will ever accept transgender people, and I'm not willing to sacrifice drag queens to the menace that is Fox News just to save the skin of trans people who don't do drag. Again, I'll have to dissect it more later.

Democratic Pushback

The largely lackluster response of the Democratic campaign to, like, any of this is nearly as frustrating as listening to Vance try to interact with a normal fast-food worker. The DNC has so effectively flopped on the messaging that I'm surprised Harris let them have a single say. Staples like "we're not going back," "weird," and "mind your damn business" have been gone for well over a month. Instead it's useless stump speech slops pushed by some politician's consultant friend hoping that this gets him a good multi-million dollar job lobbying for fucking Monsanto or something.

A couple Democratic Senate candidates have fallen prey to GOP fearmongering about "boys in girl's sports", a claim backed up by such evidence as a woman who was on HRT for years winning a swimming competition, an Algerian boxer with a bit too much muscle to pass as a woman despite her suing multiple people for defamation (including Rowling), and a movie Stephen Crowder created and had to fake because it turns out the entire premise of the movie was so bullshit not a single amateur sports league would let him try it. The Senate candidates in Ohio and Texas both said they weren't in favor of trans people playing in a sports division of their chosen gender, not even mentioning if they would be alright with it after two to three years of HRT. Seeing that, along with Hillary Clinton in 2022 or 2023 saying giving up on trans rights might be politically expedient for the Democratic Party is intensely frustrating.

However, there has been actual pushback from Harris and Walz on this. Harris did an interview on Fox News, and while the interviewer as about as rude and abrasive as you could be, as he was clearly looking for soundbytes and gotchas instead of policy, Harris actually did a good job shooting down the anti-trans rhetoric from Baier and the ads mentioned earlier. Walz pushed back even harder on a podcast appearance the next day. It was a much needed reassurance after several weeks of frustration from myself and other trans people. I'm not sure it will be enough, but anti-trans rhetoric has cost a lot of Republicans their elections in the past two years. I hope this one sticks as well.

Now if the Democrats could stop being fucking worthless when talking about immigration in any capacity whatso-fucking-ever, that would be great.
